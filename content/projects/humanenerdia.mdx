## Problem

Industrial energy management remains labor-intensive and technically demanding. Facility managers must manually interpret complex energy data, navigate regulatory requirements (ISO 50001), and make optimization decisions without intelligent support. Existing EnMS platforms lack accessible interfaces — no voice interaction, no conversational guidance, and no intelligent analytics to surface actionable insights.

## My Role & Responsibilities

- **Core Developer and System Integrator** for the full HumanEnerDIA platform
- Built and integrated the OVOS voice assistant for hands-free energy data queries
- Implemented RASA-based chatbot for text-based EnMS guidance and ISO 50001 workflow assistance
- Developed FastAPI analytics backend with time-series aggregation and anomaly detection
- Integrated LLM capabilities for natural language energy report generation
- Designed Node-RED data pipelines connecting IoT sensors to TimescaleDB
- Created Grafana dashboards for real-time energy monitoring

## Tech Stack

- **Voice AI:** OVOS (Open Voice OS) — custom skills for energy queries
- **Chatbot:** RASA — domain-specific NLU for EnMS guidance
- **Backend:** FastAPI (Python), LLM integrations for report generation
- **Data Pipeline:** Node-RED, MQTT
- **Database:** TimescaleDB (time-series optimized PostgreSQL)
- **Visualization:** Grafana with custom energy dashboards
- **Infrastructure:** Docker, Docker-Compose

## Results & Impact

- **Targets 30% reduction in technical effort** for energy management tasks through voice and chat interfaces
- **ISO 50001 alignment** — chatbot provides guided workflows for compliance documentation
- **Human-centric design** — reduced barrier to entry for facility operators who lack deep technical expertise in energy analytics
- Production-quality microservices architecture ready for field deployment

## Challenges & Lessons Learned

- **OVOS custom skill development** required deep understanding of the voice pipeline and intent handling — documentation was sparse, requiring source code reading and community engagement
- **Balancing RASA and LLM approaches** — used RASA for structured, deterministic workflows (ISO compliance steps) and LLMs for flexible natural language queries and report generation
- **Time-series data volume** — TimescaleDB's chunking and compression features were critical for handling high-frequency sensor data without query performance degradation

## How AI/Agents Were Used

LLM integration serves two purposes in this project: (1) end-user facing — generating natural language energy reports and answering operator queries about energy consumption patterns; (2) development acceleration — using AI-augmented workflows to rapidly prototype FastAPI endpoints and Node-RED flow configurations before manual validation and hardening.
